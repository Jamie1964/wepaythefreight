Concert Details

Artist: Bruce Springsteen and The E Street Band

Concert Name: Live in New York City

Year: 2000

Venue: Madison Square Garden (New York, NY)

Date: June & July 2000 (Compiled from a 10-night stand)

Background & Production Notes

Live in New York City documents the triumphant culmination of Bruce Springsteen and The E Street Band's 1999-2000 Reunion Tour. This marked their first major tour together in 11 years, reestablishing one of the most powerful live combinations in rock history. The film was recorded over the final two nights of an epic, sold-out 10-night stand at New York's historic Madison Square Garden.

The production was captured expertly by Emmy-winning director Chris Hilkene, utilizing a multi-camera high-definition setup that brought the unparalleled intimacy and stadium-level energy of the arena directly to the screen. The set captures a legendary band playing with a renewed sense of purpose, delivering definitive, high-velocity versions of core catalog tracks alongside powerful new material.

Historical Significance

This performance remains a milestone marker for the band, proving that their collective musical chemistry had only grown richer with time. The residency became cultural lore in New York City, drawing thousands of dedicated fans from all over the globe.

The concert is especially noted for its emotional depth and social commentary, featuring the highly controversial live debut of "American Skin (41 Shots)," alongside structural mainstays like "Born to Run," "Badlands," and "The Promised Land." It stands as a timeless historical record of the E Street Band's enduring live power.

Fun Facts & Trivia

  • The 10-night run at Madison Square Garden sold out completely within hours of tickets going on sale.
  • This broadcast won two Emmy Awards for its outstanding technical direction, lighting design, and sound mixing.
  • The track "Land of Hope and Dreams" was introduced on this tour as a definitive anthem for the reunited band's next chapter.
  • Guitarist Steven Van Zandt and saxophonist Clarence Clemons deliver some of their most celebrated live interaction moments during this specific New York stand.

Band Member Spotlight

Bruce Springsteen — Lead vocals, guitar, harmonica. The ultimate showman, driving the performance with unmatched passion and intensity.
Clarence Clemons — Saxophone, percussion, vocals. The "Big Man" whose booming solos define the signature E Street sound.
