Album Details

Artist: Bone Thugs-N-Harmony

Album Name: Creepin on ah Come Up

Year: 1994

Record Label: Ruthless Records

Release Date: June 21, 1994

Background & Production Notes

Released in the summer of 1994, Creepin on ah Come Up is the breakthrough debut EP that introduced Cleveland's Bone Thugs-N-Harmony to the global hip hop landscape. After auditioning backstage for N.W.A co-founder Eazy-E on a tour stop, the group was instantly signed to Ruthless Records. Eazy-E took on the role of executive producer, steering their raw talent directly into the legendary G-Funk production style dominant at the time.

The project was largely produced by DJ Yella, Rhythm D, and local beatsmiths, marrying West Coast heavy bass grooves with the group’s completely unique Midwest style. This recording capture effectively altered the trajectory of rap music, introducing a hyper-fast tongue-twisting flow mixed flawlessly with rich, layered vocal harmonies that had never been executed with such precision in gangsta rap.

Historical Significance

This release marked the birth of a brand new sub-genre. Bone Thugs-N-Harmony successfully bridged the gap between traditional rhythm and blues harmony groups and hard-hitting street rap. The EP's major breakout singles, "Thuggish Ruggish Bone" and "Foe tha Love of $," received heavy rotation across video networks and radio, helping the EP achieve multi-platinum certification.

Crucially, this project stands as one of the final iconic works associated with Eazy-E before his tragic passing in early 1995. The chemistry between his classic, sinister West Coast swagger and Bone's rapid-fire delivery created a dark, atmospheric masterpiece that defined the underground summer aesthetic of 1994.

Fun Facts & Trivia

  • Before signing with Ruthless and changing their name, the group initially performed under the moniker "B.O.N.E. Enterpri$e."
  • The tracks were recorded in Los Angeles right during the height of the mid-90s hip hop boom.
  • Singer Shatasha Williams provided the legendary soaring hook on "Thuggish Ruggish Bone," creating one of the most recognizable intros in rap history.
  • "Foe tha Love of $" prominently features an unforgettable guest verse from Eazy-E himself, acting as a passing of the torch to his new star artists.
