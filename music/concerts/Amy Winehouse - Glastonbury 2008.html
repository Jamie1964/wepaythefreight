Concert Details

Artist: Amy Winehouse

Concert Name: Live at Glastonbury

Year: 2008

Venue: Worthy Farm, Pyramid Stage (Glastonbury, UK)

Date: June 28, 2008

Background & Performance Notes

Amy Winehouse’s 2008 appearance at the Glastonbury Festival remains one of the most talked-about and memorable sets in the festival's modern history. Performing on the legendary Pyramid Stage on a warm Saturday evening just before headliners Jay-Z and The Verve, Winehouse drew a massive, sprawling crowd estimated at over 80,000 festival-goers.

Backed by her incredibly tight, sharp-dressed soul-revival touring band, Winehouse delivered a raw, emotive, and high-energy performance. Her setlist heavily leaned on tracks from her multi-platinum masterpiece album Back to Black, showcasing her unmistakable mix of jazz vocal phrasing, classic Motown sensibilities, and deeply personal storytelling.

Historical Significance

This concert captured Amy Winehouse at the absolute peak of her global cultural influence. Having won five Grammy Awards earlier that year, she arrived at Worthy Farm as an international icon. The performance highlights her unparalleled ability to command a massive festival crowd with nothing but a microphone and pure vocal talent.

Despite intense media scrutiny surrounding her personal life at the time, her musical delivery during the set was fierce. Songs like "Rehab", "Back to Black", and her vibrant rendition of "Valerie" got the entire festival grounds dancing and singing along, cementing her reputation as one of the defining voices of her generation.

Fun Facts & Trivia

  • During the performance of "Rehab," Amy famously walked down to the front barrier to interact directly with the front row of fans.
  • Her performance of "Valerie" (originally by The Zutons) became one of the ultimate sing-along moments of the entire 2008 festival line-up.
  • The set took place right before Jay-Z’s historic headlining performance later that night.
  • Amy’s signature beehive hairstyle and winged eyeliner at this show became a defining visual archive image for 2000s music culture.

Band Member Spotlight

Amy Winehouse — Lead vocals. Known for her deep contralto vocals, emotional honesty, and historic cross-genre impact.
