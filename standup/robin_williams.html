Biography

Robin Williams was an American stand‑up comedian, actor, and one of the most electrifying performers in entertainment history. Known for his rapid‑fire improvisation, boundless energy, and unmatched comedic brilliance, Williams became a global icon through stand‑up, television, and film. His work continues to inspire comedians and audiences around the world.

Career Highlights

  • Breakout role as Mork in Mork & Mindy.
  • Won an Academy Award for Good Will Hunting.
  • Legendary stand‑up specials including Live on Broadway.
  • Voiced the Genie in Disney’s Aladdin.
  • Starred in classics like Mrs. Doubtfire and Dead Poets Society.

Fun Facts

  • Known for improvising entire scenes on the spot.
  • Studied at Juilliard alongside Christopher Reeve.
  • Was an avid gamer and loved The Legend of Zelda.
  • Performed stand‑up throughout his entire career.
  • Considered one of the greatest comedic minds of all time.
