Biography

Dave Attell is an American stand‑up comedian known for his dark humor, razor‑sharp writing, and legendary late‑night performances. He rose to fame as the host of Comedy Central’s cult‑classic series Insomniac with Dave Attell, where he explored cities after hours and met the people who keep nightlife alive.

Attell is widely regarded as one of the greatest joke writers of all time, influencing generations of comedians with his precision, timing, and fearless approach to comedy. His specials — including Skanks for the Memories and Road Work — are considered some of the best in modern stand‑up.

Known for his gravelly voice, quick improvisation, and unmatched crowd work, Attell remains a staple of the New York comedy scene and a favorite among both fans and fellow comedians.
