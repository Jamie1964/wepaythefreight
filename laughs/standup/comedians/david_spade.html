Biography

David Spade is an American comedian, actor, and writer known for his sharp sarcasm, quick wit, and iconic deadpan delivery. He rose to fame on Saturday Night Live in the 1990s, where his “Hollywood Minute” and snarky persona made him a standout cast member.

Spade went on to star in hit films such as Tommy Boy, Joe Dirt, Grown Ups, and Black Sheep, becoming one of the most recognizable comedic voices of his generation. He also starred in sitcoms like Just Shoot Me! and Rules of Engagement.

With decades of stand‑up, film, and TV success, Spade remains a beloved figure in comedy, known for his effortless timing and signature sarcastic charm.
